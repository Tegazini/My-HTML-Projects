CHAPTER THREE

SWOT ANALYSIS OF NIMR

3.1 STRENGTH:

  • The only medical research institute in Nigeria.
  • Staff ensured the implementation of all policies.
  • Staff ensured there was proper communication within all units as they were interrelated.
  • Staff were knowledgeable about what they were doing.
  • The laboratory was always cleaned, and disinfection was ensured during every process.
  • Staff were open to new ideas on health advocacy, public health, and communication.
  • Visionary board leadership.
  • Available infrastructure for research.

3.2 WEAKNESSES:

  • Inadequate funding by government.
  • Low relevance to national health priorities.
  • Lack of awareness of value research to private sector.
  • Few high- quality researchers.
  • Lack of marketing of NIMR products and services.
  • Lack of awareness of NIMR activities among the Nigerian public.
  • Poor dissemination of research findings.
  • No record of daily work output by staff.
  • Researchers pursue individual goals rather than research driven by national health priorities.
  • No formal involvement in planning and execution of national disease outbreaks.
  • Low level I.T compliance amongst staff.

3.3 OPPORTUNITIES

  • Expansion of NIMR from Medical Research Institute to Health Research Institute.
  • Enactment of the new NIMR Act.
  • High potential for Health Research in Nigeria towards national and international industry priorities.
  • Collaboration with leading international research institutes.
  • Clinical trials and vaccinology.
  • An increasingly educated and aware public that will be supportive of health research.
  • Health research to state government health programs.
  • Hands-on training and workshop for different researches.

3.4 THREATS

  • Non passage of NIMR Act.
  • Lack of potential will to support health research in Nigeria.
  • Socio-cultural beliefs preventing volunteers for research.
  • Poor national economy.
  • Global financial crisis.
  • Strong international competition in research.
